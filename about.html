Sobre mim

In 1886, McCay's parents sent him to Cleary's Business College in Ypsilanti, Michigan. While in Ypsilanti, he also received his only formal art training, from John Goodison of Michigan State Normal College (now known as Eastern Michigan University). Goodison taught him the application of the fundamentals of perspective, which he put to significant use later in his career....

He also has a place in film history for being the American cinema's first great cartoon animator. He started out as a newspapwer cartoonist (New York Herald) achieving a national reputation for his strips Little Nemo in Slumberland and Dreams of a Rarebit Fiend. Those strips were both set in the dreams of their characters and featured fantasy art that attempted to capture the look and feel of dreams. McCay's cartoons were never overwhelmingly popular, but always had a strong following because of his expressive graphic style. Newspaper pages were physically much larger then and McCay usually had a half a page to work with.
